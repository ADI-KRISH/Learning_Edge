# Chapter 1: Foundations of Natural Language Processing
Natural Language Processing (NLP) is a branch of artificial intelligence that helps computers understand, interpret, and manipulate human language. The first step in any NLP pipeline is text preprocessing. Tokenization is the process of breaking text down into words or subwords. Following tokenization, stop-word removal filters out common words like "and" or "the" that carry little semantic value. Stemming and lemmatization reduce words to their base form (e.g., "running" becomes "run"). These initial steps normalize text data so machine learning models can process it.

# Chapter 2: Text Representation and Vector Space Models
Computers cannot understand raw words, so words must be converted into numerical vectors. Bag of Words (BoW) represents text by counting word occurrences, ignoring syntax. TF-IDF (Term Frequency-Inverse Document Frequency) improves this by weighting words based on their uniqueness across documents. Modern NLP relies on dense word embeddings like Word2Vec and GloVe. These embeddings represent words in a continuous vector space where semantically similar words are positioned close to each other, capturing relationships like synonyms and analogies.

# Chapter 3: Large Language Models and Transformers
Deep learning revolutionized NLP through the Transformer architecture, which uses self-attention mechanisms to process words in parallel and capture long-range dependencies in text. Bidirectional Encoder Representations from Transformers (BERT) is trained to understand word context bidirectionally. Generative Pre-trained Transformer (GPT) models excel at generating human-like text by predicting the next word sequentially. Large Language Models (LLMs) are fine-tuned on specific datasets using Reinforcement Learning from Human Feedback (RLHF) to align their outputs with human preferences.
